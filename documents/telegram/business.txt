User_52ac9c: Rising sophomores who are thinking in doing BZ:
• keep in mind that the cores are required to take ANY concentration courses (perhaps some concentrations require 3 cores actually, instead of all 4, but not sure) - so the diff is that in other colleges you can take 1 core and unlock many concentrations, while in BZ you have to complete multiple cores to unlock concentrations
• B110 and B111 are only offered in the Fall, and are required to take B112 and B113 (the other two cores)
• If you don't take B110 and B111, you can still graduate in BZ by doing the cores next year (I myself am doing that, and will be doing all concentration courses during my last year).
• But keep in mind that before you complete the cores, you cannot take concentrations. Some BZ concentrations are only offered in Fall or in Spring, and if you leave concentrations for last year, there's a chance you cannot concentrate in your preferred concentration and have to change to a general BZ major or another concentration, in case you don't get the concentrations you need
• Keep in mind that BZ is the only major that requires Practicum (B199), which is a mandatory internship in the summer between third and fourth year (it needs to be during this period, you cannot claim experiences done before this specific summer between 3rd and 4th year). It's chill but raises some common questions I wish I knew before:

About B199 (practicum) [these are not downsides, just considerations and facts, they are not negative. So keep in mind the following is just to clear up common confusions, and it's not a list of downsides - they are neutral facts]: 
• Unlike IL199, which are free (they won't charge you money for the credits you take), B199 is not free (it will consume from your allowed 120 credits that your tuition covers). If you go above 120 credits, you have to pay extra tuition to cover these credits. This is ofc not a problem bc B199 is accounted for in this allowance, but keep in mind if you're falling short on credits that you will be charged for extra credits and B199 is counted towards this allowance. This information is not useful to everyone. If you're not falling short in credits, you don't have to worry. If you are, keep in mind that you have to count B199 as a regular class (regarding it consuming your allowance of 120 credits covered by tuition/fin aid)
• CTD support in finding internships for B199 is exactly the same for all students, BZ or non-BZ, if you're wondering (ik this was a common confusion when M27s were in first year, bc Mienrva says "there's support to find internships for B199", which is kinda ambiguous - many of us didn't know whether they mean there will be specific support for B199, or if it's the same support we already have anyways at any time and any major. So if this is a confusion for you guys as well, pls know the support from CTD is the same, there are no extra tasks during the semester, it's the same coaching you can get during any other year and in any other major, and you have to be proactive in scheduling the meetings with you coach (like any student)
