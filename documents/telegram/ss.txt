User_8e115b: Something thats obviously true:
People pick university major based on interest and payoff

Something that is NOT obviously true:
Thats the only 2 reasons, or which one is more important.

Eg other possible reasons are: what majors are easy/has good profs/societaly seen positive etc

So if your paper only needs that those two reasons are considerations (eg "for many interest and payoff is important in major choice. This paper will explain how we can influence student interest") then you don't really need evidence (but would still be nice to have evidence, even if its not central, eg "based on this random research interest is one of the top 3 reasons")

But if those being the only two reasons is more central to your paper then you need better stats to back it up (eg "students only ever pick majors because of interest or payoff. We cant affect interest easily so we must affect payoff. Here's how")

--- Conversation 181 (2023-12-14) ---
User_7facf1: Definition 1. Ostrom defines an action situation: "Thus, an action situation refers to the social space where participants with diverse preferences interact, exchange goods and services, solve problems, dominate one another, or fight..." (p.14, Chapter 1, Understanding Institutional Diversity). 

Definition 2. "Whenever two or more individuals are faced with a set of potential actions that jointly produce outcomes, these individuals can be said to be “in” an action situation. " (p. 32, Chapter 2, Understanding Institutional Diversity)

Ctrl+f in this book to find more uses of action situation (there are nearly 300 references to it through out the book). Link (https://libgen.li/adsde4a3a0cb8a50f800db14609bc9ac696U0IWCTV1).

In simpler words an action space is the range of possible actions which agents can take. So it can be clear to see how rules shape the range of possible actions. 

For an application of #FormalRules for example, you want to a) use the ADICO syntax/Ostrom's categories to define the rules according to their AIM, and b) describe the action space created for agent interactions. 
 
Use Table 7.1 from Chapter 5 (https://course-resources.minerva.edu/uploaded_files/mke/00232464-1712/understanding-institutional-diversity.pdf) to see what is regulated in an action situation. An example sentence I've used is "As a regulator of choice within the action-situation, this rule has one main implication...."

Hope this helps understanding what an action space/action situation is.

User_52ac9c: My thoughts on SS162 (Personal and Social Motivation) one semester after taking that class (for future minervans who will be thinking whether to take this class on course lottery)

Before I took this class, I heard it's very bullshitable, which is partially true. However, being bullshitable doesn't mean you have to bullshit or that the topics are bullshit. If you take the class seriously and go deep into the theories (at least think about their aplicability in real life cases), you can get really good value from this class. At the time I took the class, I found the topics very interesting, but I still struggled to see the scope of the value I would take from the concepts. Now, one semester later, I can say the value is actually very strong, especially if you are going into any field that heavily depends on design thinking (basically almost everything), education, leadership, etc (basically any area that involves social interactions and behavioral analysis)

From the classes I took from the psychology series (keep in mind I only took SS110, SS162, and currently taking SS142), this is the most useful class I've had for projects outside Minerva academics. I actually applied what I learned in this class in both my civic project, and a short-time internship from outside MInerva. Thus, if you enjoy learning about human behavior and your future or current endeavors involve enhancing UX, motivating/influencing, or understanding consumer behavior, this class is really useful (but you must actually study - it is possible to pass through the class w/ a mid grade without doing the readings, especially if you get an easy grader. However, if you do so, the class will be useless. At least engage with the topics a bit (summarize the readings, search abt the topics, whatever) as long as you actually try to learn. If you do learn to engage with social psychology during this class, you can get a lot of value from it.

Keep in mind the class teaches you to apply social psychology theories and models, and won't teach you "exact" things, such as "you HAVE to do THAT in order for THIS to happen", because social psychology is relatively new and absolutely NONE of the theories are 100% proven or 100% accurate (this is taught in the course). This doesn't mean they aren't useful and doesn't mean you have to bet on one of the theories and hope it's the right one for your situation - if you use them in groupings (use diff frameworks simultaneously), you are more likely to achieve positive results. In other words, it's like complex system, you cannot change an element and expect an exact result, you can only influence the outcome, and the real value from the class (besides learning diff motivation theories) is learning how to apply different theories together to optimize your chances of influencing the outcome to the direction you are targetting.
