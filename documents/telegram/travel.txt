User_52ac9c: Sharing here in case it can be helpful for ppl who will go to BA in the future:

Tips to save money while exiting Argentina (purchasing flights):

Argentinian airports charge a variety of fees which may significantly increase the cost of flight tickets exiting Argentina, so you may notice that probably your arrival ticket is way cheaper than the ticket to exit Argentina, so I was searching a bit and found some possible solutions in case anyone else is interested in lowering the costs of exiting the country:

- If you have miles and you wish to use them, use them to issue your exit ticket (not the entry ticket). This way you avoid a bit the high costs of the exit tickets from Argentina. A practical example: a flight with lugagge from Sao Paulo to Buenos Aires is around $92, the same route but the opposite direction (BA to SP) and with luggage as well is $343 (same airline). To issue a flight with miles from SP to BA, considering United miles in this example, is 17.500 miles + 10 USD fee, but to issue the opposite direction (BA to SP) is the same amount of miles (17.500) + 86USD fee. Even tho the fee is much higher, the overall cost is cheaper if you pay the entry ticked with money and the exit ticket with miles (plus fee), because you avoid the $343 price (unless you have enough miles to issue both directions, entry and exit. In this case, it's even cheaper). Other destinations in the world, i'd guess most destinations, also have similar issues with price (the entry is cheaper than exiting Argentina).

- For some destinations, departing from Argentina might be double the price than departing from Brazil (more specifically Sao Paulo, not anywhere in Brazil - that's an important detail to notice), so you might want to consider flying to Brazil first, and then to your final destination when you exit Argentina (make sure to check if you need a visa and apply on time - even with visa costs [if any] + flight to Brazil, the overall cost might be cheaper than departing directly from Argentina. Those who go to Japan for example, a flight from BA is double the price of a flight from SP, so flying to Sao Paulo first is definitely worth it.

- Even if you don't have miles, purchasing a ticket to exit Argentina to Brazil, even tho expensive compared to the price of Brazil to Argentina, might (probably will) still be worth it and you will probably save more considering that the price from Sao Paulo to your final destination will be much cheaper (but ofc, search first before making such decision). However, if you want to save even further, you can issue the ticket from Argentina to Brazil with miles and just pay the 86 USD fee (plus the miles). If you don't have miles, but still want to save even more, and if you don't mind long travel times, you could also consider exiting Argentina to Brazil by bus (thus avoiding the high airport fees), and then getting a flight from Sao Paulo to your final destination. That would probably allow you to save even more - but I haven't checked the prices of buses, so search first. Sometimes, a flight is cheaper or the same price of a bus, so check if that's the case. However, because of high cost of fees in Argentinian airports, a bus to exit Argentina to Brazil might be cheaper (and is an alternative to issuing a flight with miles). The opposite isn't (getting a bus from Brazil to Argentina is the same price or more expensive than a flight).
However, to be honest even if you pay the full price of a flight from Argentina to Sao Paulo, without any miles, you might still save a lot of money because the flights from GRU airport in Sao Paulo are one of the cheapest in South America.

I recommend checking if your final destination will be cheaper flying from Sao Paulo before getting a return ticket from Argentina. If it is, see how cheaper it is and if it's worth it to fly to Brazil (to Sao Paulo, not anywhere in Brazil) and then depart from there (it usually is).
